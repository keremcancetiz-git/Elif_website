Designing from
Research to Reality

A user-centered designer interested in shaping services and experiences. Through research and behavioural insights, I explore how people interact with products and systems, identify pain points, and translate these insights into intuitive solutions that respond to real needs.

Methodology

Research-Led

I start with user behavior and context through interviews, diary studies, and in-situ observation to shape grounded design decisions.

Data-Enabled

I use behavioral data and system feedback loops to design services that adapt to users over time, not just at one moment.

Stakeholder Focused

I map the full service ecosystem, aligning policy, technology, and lived user experience across stakeholders.

About Me.

I see myself as a reflective thinker before a designer. I am interested in how data and research can inform design decisions and help uncover what people actually need. I enjoy exploring emerging technologies while keeping my work grounded in real experiences and everyday use.

Reflection is an important part of my process. It helps me question assumptions, understand my values and how I relate to the world around me, and stay mindful of how my decisions influence the final design. My curiosity and attention to detail allow me to dig deeper into how people interact with products and services.

I am especially drawn to design research. I believe small details, the ones that are often overlooked, can shape how a product or service is experienced.

I also believe confidence comes from allowing yourself to be a beginner and committing to giving your best no matter what. Because of this, I am always open to learning and new experiences. In teams, I adapt easily to different perspectives and enjoy being a part of the early exploration and concept development stages.
